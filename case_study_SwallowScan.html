SwallowScan - CASE STUDY

Advancing Dysphagia Diagnosis Through AI and Computer Vision Technologies

SwallowScan introduces a new standard in dysphagia diagnostics, tackling clinical complexity with advanced AI and smart visual tools. By simplifying the once labor-intensive videofluoroscopy process, SwallowScan empowers healthcare professionals with precise, rapid insights and an interactive diagnostic experience. This case study outlines the transformative impact of SwallowScan on oropharyngeal dysphagia evaluation workflows.

CLIENT

Dominic Bellino

COUNTRY

USA

INDUSTRY

Medical

Client Overview

SwallowScan is an advanced desktop-based diagnostic platform designed for the medical field, particularly for diagnosing and evaluating oropharyngeal dysphagia. Dysphagia is a complex and often underdiagnosed swallowing disorder that may result from stroke, neurological diseases, or structural anomalies. The application empowers speech-language pathologists and radiologists to make rapid and accurate assessments using AI-driven analysis of fluoroscopic videos (videofluoroscopy). SwallowScan simplifies what was previously an intricate, labor- intensive process into a seamless, highly accurate, and interactive digital experience.

The Challenge

Diagnosing dysphagia is an exceptionally complex medical challenge, both clinically and technically. Traditionally, videofluoroscopy was used, where radiologists and clinicians manually reviewed fluoroscopic videos frame by frame to assess a wide range of biomechanical indicators, including but not limited to:

➤

The displacement and trajectory of the hyoid bone, critical for airway protection and bolus propulsion.

➤

The movement and clearance of the bolus as it travels through the oral, pharyngeal, and Hypopharynx regions.

➤

The level of residual bolus post-swallow, indicating risk of aspiration.

➤

The degree of pharyngeal constriction and muscle efficiency.

➤

The structural alignment of vertebrae and their relation to swallowing mechanics.

➤

Reference object (calibration ring) detection for accurate measurement scaling.

This manual analysis process was time-consuming, subject to inter-observer variability, and highly prone to human error. Further complications included:

➤

Inconsistencies in anatomical landmark detection.

➤

No standardized mechanism for converting pixel-based measurements to clinical metrics.

➤

Lack of automated tools and digital storage of patient data.

➤

Absence of dashboards for real-time graphical analysis.

➤

No integration between measurement tools and patient record systems.

As dysphagia evaluation requires multi-metric and temporal analysis, traditional workflows made it difficult for clinicians to analyze large volumes of data or revisit historical patterns, delaying treatment and impacting accuracy.

Solution Approach

SwallowScan introduces a powerful AI-enhanced desktop application that automates and augments the entire dysphagia assessment pipeline using cutting- edge computer vision technologies. Unlike traditional tools, SwallowScan supports both manual and automated workflows, giving medical practitioners full control and flexibility.

1. AI-Powered Multi-Model Detection & Segmentation:

SwallowScan integrates multiple machine learning models to intelligently detect and track relevant anatomical regions:

Mask R-CNN (Segmentation):

Used for precise delineation of:

Bolus segmentation and residue analysis

Throat area mapping

Calibration ring (circle detection for unit conversion)

YOLOv5 (Object Detection):

Used for fast and reliable detection of:

Hyoid bone (initial and displaced positions)

Cervical vertebrae (structural markers for alignment)

Step-by-Step Workflow:

Video Acquisition: Doctors upload fluoroscopic videos (x-ray format) of patients swallowing nectar-thick syrup.

Frame Extraction: Each video is decomposed into sequential frames for granular analysis.

Landmark Detection: Ring, hyoid bone, and vertebrae are located and tracked.

Bolus Segmentation & Analysis: Key calculations are performed: Bolus Clearance Ratio (BCR) Hyoid Bone Displacement (HD) Pharyngeal Constriction Ratio (PCR)

Post-Swallow Residue Identification for accurate diagnostic insights.

Graphical Visualization of biomechanical motion (hyoid trajectory plotting).

Pressure Monitoring for PES Max using sensor data (if available).

Automated Calculations:

SwallowScan computes and exports several diagnostic metrics:

BCR = (Residual Area / Total Bolus Area) * 100

PCR = ((Total Bolus Area - Residual Area) / Total Bolus Area) * 100

Hyoid Displacement = Initial - Displaced Position (in mm)

Post-Swallow Residue Identification for accurate diagnostic insights.

Pixel-to-mm Conversion = Pixels / Calibrated Ring Diameter

PES Max = Pressure (mmHg) / 10

Swallow Initiation Time

Velocity of Bolus Propagation

Feature-Rich and User-Centric System:

Manual override of AI predictions for clinician input.

Role-based access with secure doctor login/signup.

Patient record tracking and comparison across multiple visits.

Interactive, real-time UI for both visual and quantitative analysis.

Exportable graphs, data tables, and diagnostic summaries.

Option to switch between automated and manual workflows for flexibility.

The Outcome

SwallowScan has successfully redefined dysphagia diagnostics through a combination of automation, AI intelligence, and human-centered design. Key benefits include:

Efficiency

Analysis that once took hours can now be done in seconds.

Accuracy

Deep-learning-based anatomical landmark detection significantly reduces human error.

Comprehensiveness

From bolus to hyoid to vertebrae—every relevant metric is covered.

Flexibility

Manual mode allows clinicians to review or adjust results when needed.

Comparability

Standardized mm/cm outputs enable comparison across patient timelines.

Scalability

Built-in patient history and secure storage support long-term usage in clinical environments.

Clinical Intelligence

Graphs and quantitative metrics support evidence- based decision making.

Adaptability

The system is designed for future integration with hospital EHR systems, remote diagnostics, and telehealth support.

By addressing a deeply intricate clinical problem with intelligent automation and flexibility, SwallowScan has created a new benchmark in the diagnosis and monitoring of dysphagia.
